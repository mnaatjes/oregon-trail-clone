# Game Mechanics (Apple II - 1985)

This document details the mechanics of the 1985 Apple II version of The Oregon Trail.

## 1. Travel Mechanics
- **Daily Progress:** The game calculates distance traveled each day based on pace, weather, and number of healthy oxen.
- **Pace Settings:**
    - **Steady:** 8 hours of travel per day.
    - **Strenuous:** 12 hours per day.
    - **Grueling:** 16 hours per day. Higher risk of exhaustion and injury.
- **Rations Settings:**
    - **Filling:** High food consumption, improves health.
    - **Meager:** Moderate consumption.
    - **Bare Bones:** Low consumption, causes health to decline.

## 2. Resource Management
- **Oxen Management:** Having more oxen allows for faster travel in difficult terrain. Oxen can die from exhaustion or disease.
- **Maintenance:** Breakdowns occur randomly. If the player lacks a spare part, they must spend days repairing it or trade for one.

## 3. Mini-Games
- **Hunting:**
    - Triggered by the player via the menu.
    - The player controls the hunter with keyboard arrows and uses space to shoot.
    - **The 100lb Rule:** Even if the player kills 2000lbs of buffalo, they can only carry 100lbs back to the wagon.
- **River Crossing:**
    - **Ford:** Attempting to pull the wagon through. Successful if water is shallow.
    - **Caulk and Float:** Sealing the wagon. Successful if the current isn't too strong.
    - **Ferry:** Safest but costs money.
- **Columbia River Rafting:**
    - The final challenge. Players must steer a raft through rapids, avoiding rocks. Crashing loses supplies or party members.

## 4. Health and Survival
- **Disease Generation:** Random events based on health status and weather.
- **Resting:** Players can choose to "Stop to Rest" for several days to improve health at the cost of food and time.
